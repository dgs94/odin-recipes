Pulled Pork

Description

A delicious and simple pulled pork recipe that be slow cooked on game day. Tender and melt-in-your-mouth, this recipe is perfect on a soft bun, tacos, nachos or just on its own. Plus it takes no effort at all!

Ingredients

  • (3-4lb) Pork Shoulder (fresh or thawed)
  • (x2 tbsp) Oil (Ideally a neutral oil such as avacado, grapeseed or peanut)
  • (x3 tbsp) Kosher salt
  • (x1 tbsp) Pepper
  • (x1 tbsp) Garlic Powder
  • (1/2) large Sweet Onion
  • (x1) 2L bottle of Dr.Pepper (or cola of your choice)
  • (x1) Bottle Sweet Baby Rays BBQ Sauce (or your favourite BBQ sauce)

Steps

  1. Prepare the pork shoulder by trimming any excess fat from the roast. (Too much fat won't render, leaving an overly greasy finished product)
  2. Pat the roast dry with a paper towel.
  3. Evenly coat the pork with the oil.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the salt, pepper and garlic.
  5. Sprinkle the pork with the seasoning mix and rub the mix into all areas of the meat to evenly cover.
  6. Cut the onion into 1/4" thick slices using a mandolin or sharp knife.
  7. Line the bottom of your slow cooker with a bed of onions.
  8. Place the pork shoulder into the slow cooker and top with remaining onions.
  9. At the side of the slow cooker, pour roughly 3 cups of Dr.Pepper to fill the slow cooker 3/4 of the way.
  10. Brush exposed pork with bbq sauce.
  11. Top slow cooker with lid and cook on low for 8 hours or high for 4.
  12. A couple of times throughout the cook, rotate and rebrush with bbq sauce as needed.
  13. Pork shoulder is done when it is tender and easily pulled with a fork.
  14. Strain the liquid from the cooker and using two forks shred the meat.
  15. Mix with shredded pork with bbq sauce as desired.
  16. Bon Appetit!
